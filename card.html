Blobfish

The popular impression of the blobfish as bulbous and gelatinous is partially an artifact of the decompression damage done to specimens when they are brought to the surface from the extreme depths in which they live.

  • Scientific Name: Psychrolutes marcidus
  • Average Length: 30 cm
  • Average Lifespan: Up to 130 years
  • Habitat: Deep waters of coasts of Australia and New Zealand

Psychrolutes marcidus, aslo know simply as blobfish is a deep sea fish of the family Psychrolutidae. They live at depths between 600 and 1,200 m (2,000 and 3,900 ft) where the pressure is 60 to 120 times as great as at sea level. The flesh of the blobfish is primarily a gelatinous mass with a density slightly less than water; this allows the fish to float above the sea floor without expending energy on swimming. Its relative lack of muscle is not a disadvantage as it primarily swallows edible matter that floats in front of it such as deep-ocean crustaceans.
